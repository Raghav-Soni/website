Raghav Soni

I am a senior undergraduate student of Electronics Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology (BHU), Varanasi. I work actively with different research labs on projects centered around robotics, controls, reinforcement learning and computer vision.

Most recently, I worked under the supervision of Dr. Shivesh Kumar, at the Robotics Innovation Center, German Research Center for artificial Intelligence to develop deep reinforcement learning based controllers for underactuated systems like hopping leg, simple pendulum and acrobot. Earlier, I worked as a student researcher under the joint supervision of Dr. Shishir Kolathaya and Dr. Ayonga Hereid at the Stochastic Robotics Lab, Indian Institute of Science. Here we worked on developing lightweight template model based controllers for diverese bipedal gaits using reinforcement learning. Prior to that, I worked at the Artificial Intelligence and Mechatronics Lab, IIT Bhubaneswar under the supervision of Dr. Pandu Ranga Vundavilli to implement LIP trajectories on a humanoid robot with active vision based control.

Along with my research activities, I love to work on algorithms, listen to music and play football. I also lead a robotics research based group, RoboReG at IIT (BHU). I spend a lot of time mentoring fellow students and served as the Joint Secretary of the Robotics Club, IIT (BHU). I am always looking forward to challenges, exploring new domains and connecting with people.

Research

I am interested in optimal control, reinforcement learning, optimization, and computer vision. My research work focuses on developing robust controllers for legged robotic systems. I look forward to development of resilient vision based controllers for legged robotic systems that would enable them to be deployed in human-centric enviroments. Representative works are highlighted.

Publications
MELP: Model Embedded Linear Policies for Robust Bipedal Hopping
Raghav Soni, Guillermo A. Castillo, Lokesh Krishna, Ayonga Hereid, Shishir Kolathaya,
ICRA, 2023 (Under Review)
Paper / Video

We show how to embed Spring Loaded Inverted Pendulum (SLIP) model in the proposed linear policy class and realize perpetual hopping in arbitrary directions.

End-to-End Reinforcement Learning for Torque Based Variable Height Hopping
Raghav Soni, Daniel Harnack, Shivesh Kumar, Frank Kirchner
ICRA, 2023 (Under Review)
Paper / Video

We present a end-to-end RL based torque controller that learns to implicitly detect the relevant jump phases, removing the need to provide manual heuristics for state detection. We also extend a method for simulation to reality transfer of the learned controller to contact rich dynamic tasks, resulting in successful deployment on the robot after training without parameter tuning.

Projects
Intelligent Picking : Warehouse Automation
Yash Sahijwani, Raghav Soni, Ayush Kumar Shaw, Niranth Sai, Lokesh Krishna
Flipkart Grid 2.0, National Finalists
Slides / GitHub / Video

Designed an intelligent object picking robot inspired by the 3D printer's structure in simulation. YOLOv3 was used for object detection and GR-ConvNet was used to generate robust antipodal grasps from images of a RGB-D camera.

Dynamic Linear Inverted Pendulum (LIP) trajectories with position control
Raghav Soni, Dr. Pandu Ranga Vundavilli
GitHub / Video

Developed a framework to generate dynamic Linear Inverted Pendulum (LIP) trajectories and implemented a position based controller to track these trajectories on the humanoid Thormang3 in simulation. Added stabilizing heuristics and vision algorithms for dynamic obstacle avoidance and stair climbing.

Handwritten Character Segmentation and Recognition
Raghav Soni, Yash Sahijwani, Ayush Kumar Shaw, Dr. A K Singh
Exploratory Project, Department of Electronics Engineering, IIT (BHU)
GitHub / Report

Developed a computer vision based solution to filter noise and segment characters. This was followed by a custom made CNN trained using EMNIST dataset to recognise the segmented characters.

Adversarial Chase and Run Cars
Raghav Soni, Yash Sahijwani, Ayush Kumar Shaw
Research Project, RoboReG, IIT (BHU)
GitHub / Project Page

Developed an Adversarial Chase and Run Cars custom gym environment, to test and develop algorithms related to Multi Agent Systems, especially those related to Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning. Tested various Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning algorithms including a custom version of DQN and MADDPG.

Autonomous Environment Mapping Robot
Raghav Soni, Yash Sahijwani, Vikhyath Venkatraman
Exploratory Project, Robotics Club, IIT (BHU)
GitHub / Report

Developed an autonomous wheeled robot in PyBullet simulation with custom implemented and calibrated stereo camera for depth sensing and YOLOv3 for object detection. The wheeled robot is capable of traversing the environment without colliding and map it simultanously.

Autonomous Self Driving Car in Simulation
Raghav Soni, Yash Sahijwani, Ayush Kumar Shaw
Python Notebook / Video

Developed a custom CNN inspired by Nvidia Self driving architecture capable of operating a car with throttle in Udacity's Self-driving simulation using center, left and right RGB camera images. The images were augmented using diverse techniques to make the network robust to environment conditions.

Tetris using Digital ICs
Raghav Soni, Varsha Jangir, Smriti Sharma
Simulation / Report / Video

Developed a 10x20 tetris games capable of all functionalities using just digital ICs consisting of adders, multiplexers, comparators, flip flops, and counters.
