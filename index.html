Raghav Soni

I am a senior undergraduate student of Electronics Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology (BHU), Varanasi. I work actively with different research labs on projects centered around robotics, controls, reinforcement learning and computer vision.

Most recently, I worked under the supervision of Dr. Shivesh Kumar, at the Robotics Innovation Center, German Research Center for artificial Intelligence to develop deep reinforcement learning based controllers for underactuated systems like hopping leg, simple pendulum and acrobot. Earlier, I worked as a student researcher under the joint supervision of Dr. Shishir Kolathaya and Dr. Ayonga Hereid at the Stochastic Robotics Lab, Indian Institute of Science. Here we worked on developing lightweight template model based controllers for diverese bipedal gaits using reinforcement learning. Prior to that, I worked at the Artificial Intelligence and Mechatronics Lab, IIT Bhubaneswar under the supervision of Dr. Pandu Ranga Vundavilli to implement LIP trajectories on a humanoid robot with active vision based control.

Along with my research activities, I love to work on algorithms, listen to music and play football. I also lead a robotics research based group, RoboReG at IIT (BHU). I spend a lot of time mentoring fellow students and served as the Joint Secretary of the Robotics Club, IIT (BHU). I am always looking forward to challenges, exploring new domains and connecting with people.

Research

I am interested in optimal control, reinforcement learning, optimization, and computer vision. My research work focuses on developing robust controllers for legged robotic systems. I look forward to development of resilient vision based controllers for legged robotic systems that would enable them to be deployed in human-centric enviorment.
